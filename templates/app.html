Deploy & interact with retention tontine contracts using your browser wallet.

In a traditional tontine, money is pooled among a group of people and whenever someone dies, their share is paid out to the living members of the contract. This was once a popular way to provide annuities or fund public goods like the Richmond Bridge in London.
---------------------------------------
In this retention tontine, partipants compete to hold assets such as meme coins to the end of the contract term. If someone decides to withdraw early (maybe because the asset has already appreciated 1000%) they pay a 20% penalty into a fee pool that gets distributed to the remaining holders. This penalty is applied after they collect their own share of the existing fee pool, upon withdrawal. At the end of the contract term, holders can withdraw their coins penalty-free along with their share of accumulated fees. If you are the "last man standing" you can withdraw your coins and share of fees penalty-free at any time.
----------------------------------------
You may also withdraw funds penalty-free before the end of the enrollment period for a contract and make partial withdrawals at any time.
